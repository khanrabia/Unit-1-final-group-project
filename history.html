History Timeline

  • 1970 - A same-sex couple in Minnesota applies for a marriage license and was denied. It was then transferred to the state Supreme Court.
  • 1973 - Maryland was the first state to ban same-sex marriage.
  • 1976 - A Non-church approved a gay wedding, which made news.
  • 1983 - Spousal rights of same-sex couples become an issue - a lesbian couple is confronted with the spousal rights issue when one of them is in a car accident and the other was denied the right to care for her.
  • 1984 - Berkeley, California passed the nation's first domestic partnership law.
  • 1987 - The first mass same-sex wedding ceremony occured 1n the National Mall, nearly over 2000 same-sex marriages happened.
  • 1989 - In New York and California the court ruled same-sex couples as families.
  • 1992 - same-sex employees begin to receive domestic partner benefits from Levi Strauss & Co. and the state of Mass.
  • 1993 - the Hawaii Supreme Court ruled that same-sex marriage should not be denied unless there is a convincing reason to do so. The legislators respond by passing an amendment to ban gay marriage.
  • 1995 - Utah governor signs a state Defense of Marriage Act statute into law.
  • 1996 - President Clinton signed the federal Defense of Marriage Act.
  • 1997 - Hawaii becamed the first state to offer domestic partnership benefits to same sex couples.
  • 1998 - Alaskan and Hawaiian voters approve state constitutional bans on same-sex marriage.
  • 1999 - Vermont Supreme Court rules that same-sex couples must receive the same benefits and protections as any other married couple under the Vermont Constitution.
  • 2000 - The Central Conference of American Rabbis allowed religious ceremonies for same-sex couples, Vermont became the first state to pass a law granting full benefits of marriage to same-sex couples.
  • 2002 - Nevada voted to approve a state constitutional ban on same-sex marriage.
  • 2003 - A proposed amendment to the federal Constitution was introduced to the House of Representatives.
  • 2004 - it was a debate among states to issue licenses to same-sex couples or to deny them the rights still.
  • 2005 - New York called same-sex marriage illegal. California legislature attempted to pass a law to legalize the union of same-sex marriage but was rejected by the governor, while Connecticut became the second state to allow same-sex marriage.
  • 2006 - the New Jersey Supreme Court orders the legislature to recognize same-sex unions.
  • 2008 - California, Florida and Arizona overturned the ban on gay marriage.
  • 2009 - the Iowa Supreme Court overturns the state ban on same-sex marriage. Vermonts legislature legalizes same-sex marriages. Maine and New Hampshire follow suit, though Maine voters later repeal the state law allowing same-sex marriage.
  • 2011 - President Obama declared the Defense of Marriage Act unconstitutional. New York legalizes same-sex marriage.
  • 2012 - Washington state, Maine, and Maryland legalize same-sex marriage by popular vote.
  • 2013 - Rhode Island, Delaware, Minnesota, New Jersey, Hawaii, Illinois, and New Mexico legalize same-sex marriage.
  • 2014 - Oregon, Pennsylvania, Kansas, and South Carolina legalize same-sex marriage. The Presbyterian church votes to allow same-sex ceremonies.
  • 2015 - The year the U.S. Supreme Court made same-sex marriages legal in all 50 states in Obergefell v. Hodges case.
